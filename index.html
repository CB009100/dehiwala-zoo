Welcome to Dehiwala Zoo

The National Zoological Gardens of Sri Lanka, also known as the Dehiwala Zoo, is a sprawling green oasis located just a short drive from the city's heart. It is home to a variety of fascinating animals, birds, and butterflies.

Dehiwala Zoo, which has over 2500 animals, is home to many exotic and indigenous species of mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. It is well-known for its lush green landscaped gardens and dense tree cover, in addition to its amazing variety of flora.

About Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a naturalist's paradise. Despite its small size, the island has one of the highest rates of biological endemism in the world, whether in plants or animals, and it is one of the world's top five biodiversity hotspots. Sri Lanka, like all islands, has a plethora of unique species.

With its wildly diverse habitats, including cool misty mountains, rain forests, rolling oceans and expansive beaches, and even an arid zone resembling Africa's plains, the 65,610-square-kilometer area is home to an opulent wildlife gene pool.

Fauna

With more than 13% of the country's land area dedicated to sanctuaries for fauna and flora, Sri Lanka is home to animals such as elephants, leopards, sloth bears, sambar deer, spotted deer, hogs, mouse- and barking deer, wild boars, porcupines, ant-eaters, civet cats, loris, giant squirrels, and monkeys like the macaque, purple-faced leaf monkey, and grey langur.

Flora

Natural vegetation covers roughly one-third of Sri Lanka's total land area. The lowlands of the Wet Zone are dominated by tropical wet evergreen forest, while the highlands are dominated by submontane and montane evergreen forests. The Dry Zone's climax vegetation consists of dry evergreen forest and moist deciduous forest, with forests giving way to stunted, shrubby, xerophytic (drought-tolerant) vegetation in its driest parts. Forests are sparse and interspersed with grasslands in the Central Highlands' highest areas.
