ANIMALS

Arapaima

The arapaima is one of the world's largest freshwater fish species. It has a streamlined body with an upturned mouth. It has a life expectancy of about 20 years, enabling it to grow up to 200 kg in weight and span up to 10 ft in length. The arapaima has the ability to breathe air, which allows it to live in oxygen-deficient rivers and pools with low water levels. Its diet consists of fish, insects, birds, mammals, fruits, and seeds.

Asian Elephant

For centuries, elephants have been considered sacred in Asia, as they play an important role in many cultures and religions. Asian elephants are slightly smaller than African elephants, reaching up to 10 ft in height and weigh as much as 5 tons. Compared to their African cousins, they have smaller ears which they use constantly to cool themselves. They spend more than two thirds of their day, consuming large amounts of grass, tree bark, roots, leaves, and stems. Among their favorite food are bananas, rice, and sugarcane. Asian elephants reach their full size at about 17 years and have an average life expectancy of around 45 years of age.

Crimson Rose

Scientifically know as Pachliopta hector, the crimson rose butterfly is an abundant species of butterflies, that can be seen in the dry zone and occasionally in the low country wet zone of Sri Lanka. The crimson rose is a black butterfly with two white bands on the forewing and two rows of bright crimson patches on the hindwing. This species travel in groups and are migratory. They visit the flowers of scrubs and some varieties of herbs.

Giraffe

Scientifically know as Giraffa Camelopardalis, Giraffes are the tallest animals on Earth. They can grow up to 15-18 ft tall. Males weigh up to 1900 kg, females up to 1200 kg. Giraffes consume leaves, flowers, fruits, and bark. They have 7 vertebrae in their long necks. Under human care, these gentle giants can live up to 25 years.

Marsh Crocodile

The Marsh crocodile also known as the mugger crocodile, can live in both freshwater habitats such as rivers, lakes , village ponds, man-made reservoirs, and in saltwater lagoons along the coast. This crocodile does not migrate seasonally. The marsh crocodile can grow up to 18 ft long and weigh as much as 450 kg. Marsh crocodiles prey on any animal smaller than them including other crocodiles. They can live up to 30 years in captivity.

Sri Lankan Leopard

The Sri Lankan Leopard scientifically identified as Panthera pardus kotiya is native to Sri Lanka and has been classified as an endangered subspecies. These big cats can be observed in their natural habitats at the Yala National Park and the Wilpattu National Park. The average size of this subspecies is 50-90 kg in weight and around 6 ft in length (excluding the tail). The Sri Lankan Leopard feeds on animals such as hare, Sambar deer, langurs, frogs, birds, reptiles, rodents, and insects. It has a life expectancy of 12-15 years under human care.

Orangutan

With long, graceful, reddish-orange, or dark brown hair, orangutans are the only type of great apes with a native habitat outside of Africa. The name “Orangutan” translates to “People of the forest”. Averaging from about 3-5.6 ft in height and 45-90 kg, orangutans are the largest arboreal mammals in Asia. These highly intelligent primates spend the majority of their lives in trees, swinging from branch to branch with their long arms. Their diet includes a variety of fruits, vegetables, figs, leaves, flowers, bark, insects, and honey. Orangutans can live up to 59 years in zoos.

Ostrich

Ostriches are the tallest living birds. Grasslands and deserts are their natural habitats. Ostriches feed on leaves, grass and animal remains. Fully grown adults weigh between 90-130 kg and range from 6-9 ft tall. With the use of their long and powerful legs these birds are able to run up to 60 km per hour in short bursts. Ostriches can live up to 50 years, under human care.
